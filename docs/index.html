How It Works
  • Base geography is the official General Practitioner Catchments (GPC 2023) for all of Australia.
  • DPA status comes from the official Distribution Priority Area (GPs 2025) dataset.
  • Each catchment is linked to SA2 demographic context (median age, income, growth, disadvantage).
  • Opportunity score blends official GP shortage signal (DPA) with demographic pressure indicators so users can compare where unmet demand is likely strongest.
How To Use
  • Start in All States to identify national hotspots.
  • Switch to one state to load detailed polygons and inspect local catchments.
  • Toggle the metric selector to stress-test rankings by growth, age, income, or disadvantage.
  • Click a point to open full catchment metrics and compare with chart evidence.
Map Legend In Plain English
  • Colour ramp: red = lower values, green = higher values for the selected metric.
  • Circle markers: one marker per official GP catchment.
  • Shaded polygons: official catchment boundaries for the selected state.
  • White ring: currently selected catchment.
Metric Glossary
  • DPA: Distribution Priority Area status for GPs (Y = shortage priority area).
  • SA2: Statistical Area Level 2, an ABS standard region used for local statistics.
  • IRSD: SEIFA Index of Relative Socio-economic Disadvantage.
  • Need Index: Weighted percentile mix of age, growth, low-income, and disadvantage pressure.
